Anne Lambâ€™s voyeuristic video work, entitled Surrogates, deals with her past and present experiences with heterosexual men and their delicate and often ignored issues of body image and expressed sexuality within the discourse of feminism and queer theory. In this work, the artist video tapes herself in frame with her male subjects, while they discuss her own personal experiences with romance, the limitations set upon men in regard to sexual expressions, and the animosity towards the feminist movement from the position of the heteronormative man.

Anne Lamb's prior works have dealt with feminine sexual anxiety, animal mythology, and is now exploring masculine sexual identity through voyeuristic video documentation of photo shoots. Anne Lamb graduated with a BFA from the School of Visual Arts in 2011, and has worked for artists such as Marilyn Minter, Elektra KB, and Ryan McGinley.
